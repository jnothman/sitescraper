Rate expectations stir up housing market

  • Email
  • Print
  • Normal font
  • Large font

Related Coverage

  • Fears rise that Australia's resources boom ending
  • Eli Greenblat
  • August 18, 2008

MELBOURNE'S weak property market got a shot in the arm at the weekend as speculation of a cut in official interest rates as early as next month helped to boost clearance rates and lift hopes of more aggressive bidding from formerly timid buyers.

It could lead to an upbeat spring auction season, with buyers more willing to purchase a home if they believe the current cycle of tighter interest rates is over.

Figures collected from across the metropolitan area showed clearance rates for weekend auctions rose strongly to 66%, up from 60% last weekend.

It was the biggest gain in week-on-week growth for a non-holiday auction period in a year, although the clearance rate is still well below rates close to 85% reached last year.

The highest weekend clearance rate was recorded for house auctions in the north-east (88%), and sales activity in the inner city was also strong, according to the Real Estate Institute of Victoria.

"Buyers reacted positively to speculation about a drop in interest rates by the Reserve Bank, leading to an improvement in the clearance rate," REIV chief executive Enzo Raimondo said.

Agents working at the weekend at auctions and open-for-inspections reported there was more interest from buyers. However, they said home buyers would need to see a number of rate cuts before they rushed back into the market.

Peter Bennison, a director at JP Dixon Real Estate, said bidding was not fierce at the auctions he attended in the inner east, but properties were selling rather than being passed in.

"People are starting to think about property again," Mr Bennison said. "They may not be out there bidding strongly yet, but they are starting to think this is the end."

Craig Stephens, director of sales at Jas H Stephens, said: "Buyers and sellers still seem to be sitting on the fence a bit. It seems to be a given that the RBA will reduce rates next month, but the big question on everyone's lips is 'will the banks pass on the full rate cut?'

"There is a lot of stock about to hit the market in September and October and this rate cut would certainly be very pleasing and greeted warmly by people … it will be the spark that we need for the rest of the year."

Buyers' advocate Christopher Koren, of Morrell & Koren, said there was still strong demand for good-quality homes in the best suburbs, such as Malvern, Toorak and Armadale, but some of the interest was starting to spill into second-tier properties.

"I don't think interest rates at this stage are going to make a lot of difference to a 'grade A' home … but, generally speaking, for other parts of the market there is a little bit more effervescence," Mr Koren said.
